Second Sip
Æon Teunissen, Blake Zeroven, Clara Florke
10 April – 26 April, 2026

Second Sip convenes 17 brunettes in a spatial arrangement defined by calibrated intensities, where gesture is held in a precise tension between assertion and restraint. Scale, material, and duration are continuously negotiated, producing an exhibition that does not resolve into spectacle but instead redistributes attention across a field of insistent yet withholding presences. Several works occupy the space with marked physical clarity—surfaces are rendered opaque, volumes reoriented, and luminous structures displaced from their expected hierarchies. These gestures anchor the exhibition while simultaneously diffusing focus, creating a condition in which what is encountered remains partial, contingent, and slightly out of reach.

Material is treated as a temporal register. Substances appear held in states of viscosity, residue, or near-exhaustion, while objects index moments that feel both ongoing and already elapsed. This produces a shared sense of latency: a continuous relation to what has just occurred and what cannot fully follow. Minimal means here operate not as reduction alone, but as concentration—each gesture carrying a heightened density. Light functions as a structuring principle. Natural luminosity enters insistently, diffused through altered surfaces, while artificial sources remain both infrastructural and estranged. Illumination disperses rather than directs, accumulating in gradients that destabilize fixed points of attention. In the lower register, light becomes more contained and fragmented, shifting toward a technical, almost diagrammatic condition. Sound and performance extend this logic into time. A vocal line reduced to repetition circulates as a minimal unit, while the body engages in a prolonged sequence that approaches fulfillment without resolution. Action is sustained at the threshold of completion.

Second Sip proposes a mode of attention attuned to such thresholds—between opacity and visibility, insistence and withdrawal. The “second sip” emerges as a recalibration: a return that sharpens perception while leaving the encounter unresolved.
